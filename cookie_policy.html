1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device — computer, smartphone, or tablet — when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, to improve the user experience, and to provide information to the website owners.

Cookies are not programs and cannot carry viruses or install malware on your device. They simply store a small amount of data that can be retrieved by the web server at a later time.

There are several types of cookies distinguished by their origin and lifespan:

  • First-party cookies — set directly by WanderBritain when you visit our website.
  • Third-party cookies — set by third-party services integrated into our site, such as analytics or advertising platforms.
  • Session cookies — temporary cookies that are deleted when you close your browser.
  • Persistent cookies — cookies that remain on your device for a set period of time or until you delete them manually.

2. Why We Use Cookies

WanderBritain uses cookies for several legitimate purposes, including:

  • Ensuring the website functions correctly and securely.
  • Remembering your preferences and personalising your experience.
  • Analysing how visitors interact with our website so we can improve it.
  • Measuring the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns.
  • Providing relevant advertisements based on your interests.
  • Facilitating enquiry form submissions and other interactive features.

We aim to use only those cookies that are strictly necessary or that provide a meaningful benefit to you as a visitor. Where consent is required, we will ask for it before placing cookies on your device.

9. Cookie Retention Periods

The length of time a cookie remains on your device depends on whether it is a session cookie or a persistent cookie:

  • Session cookies are temporary and are deleted automatically when you close your browser.
  • Persistent cookies remain on your device for a defined period, ranging from 1 minute to 2 years, depending on their purpose.

A summary of retention periods for specific cookies is provided in the tables above. We review our cookie usage regularly and update retention periods where necessary to ensure they remain proportionate and consistent with our stated purposes.

Most browsers allow you to view, manage, and delete cookies through their settings menus. Please note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of our website and other websites you visit. Here are links to cookie management guides for common browsers:

11. Your Consent

In accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003 (PECR), we require your consent before placing any non-essential cookies on your device.

Consent is:

  • Freely given — you are not required to consent to non-essential cookies in order to access our website.
  • Specific — you can choose which categories of cookies you accept.
  • Informed — we explain what each category of cookies does before asking for your consent.
  • Unambiguous — we use an opt-in mechanism; pre-ticked boxes are not used for non-essential cookies.

You have the right to withdraw your consent at any time. To do so, click the "Cookie Settings" link in the footer of our website and update your preferences. Withdrawing consent will not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.

For further information on how we process your personal data, please read our Privacy Policy.

12. GDPR & ePrivacy Compliance

This Cookie Policy is designed to comply with the following legislation applicable to our operations in the United Kingdom:

  • UK GDPR — The UK General Data Protection Regulation, retained in UK law following the UK's departure from the European Union, governs the processing of personal data collected via cookies.
  • PECR — The Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003 (as amended) implement the EU ePrivacy Directive in the United Kingdom and specifically regulate the use of cookies and similar tracking technologies.
  • Data Protection Act 2018 — Supplements the UK GDPR and provides further data protection requirements in the UK.

Under these regulations, the legal bases we rely upon for cookie processing are:

  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a) UK GDPR) — for analytical, functional (where not strictly necessary), and marketing cookies.
  • Legitimate Interests (Article 6(1)(f) UK GDPR) — for certain functional cookies required to operate our website securely and effectively, where our interests are not overridden by your rights.
  • Legal Obligation — where required to comply with applicable law.

We have conducted a legitimate interests assessment (LIA) for cookies processed on that basis and are satisfied that our interests are proportionate and do not override individuals' rights and freedoms.

If you have concerns about how we process your data, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO): ico.org.uk, telephone: 0303 123 1113.
