Privacy Policy

Your privacy matters to us. This policy explains what personal data WanderBritain collects, how we use it, and the rights you have under applicable law.

Last Updated:

1. Who We Are

WanderBritain ("we", "us", "our") is a travel services company registered in England and Wales. We operate as a controller of your personal data under the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

2. What Personal Data We Collect

We may collect and process the following categories of personal data when you interact with our website or services:

  • Identity Data: First name, last name, title.
  • Contact Data: Email address, telephone number, postal address.
  • Travel Preferences: Preferred destinations, travel dates, group size, dietary or accessibility requirements you choose to share.
  • Technical Data: Internet Protocol (IP) address, browser type and version, time zone setting, browser plug-in types, operating system and platform, and other technology on the devices you use to access our website.
  • Usage Data: Information about how you use our website, products, and services, including pages visited, links clicked, and time spent on pages.
  • Cookie Data: Data collected via cookies and similar tracking technologies (see Section 7 for full details).
  • Communications Data: Messages you send us via our contact forms, emails, or telephone calls.
  • Marketing Data: Your preferences in receiving marketing communications from us and our third parties.

We do not knowingly collect sensitive personal data (special category data) such as health, racial origin, or political opinions unless you voluntarily provide it (e.g., accessibility requirements for your trip).

3. How We Collect Your Data

We collect data through the following methods:

  • Direct Interactions: When you complete enquiry or contact forms, subscribe to our newsletter, call or email us, or request information about our tours.
  • Automated Technologies: As you interact with our website, we automatically collect Technical and Usage Data via cookies, server logs, and similar technologies.
  • Third Parties: We may receive data from analytics providers (such as Google Analytics), advertising networks, and social media platforms where you have chosen to interact with our content.

4. Purposes and Legal Bases for Processing

We process your personal data only where we have a valid legal basis to do so under UK GDPR. The table below explains our processing activities and their legal bases:

Purpose Legal Basis
Responding to your enquiries and booking requests Performance of a contract / Pre-contractual steps (Art. 6(1)(b))
Processing and managing travel bookings Performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b))
Sending marketing communications (newsletters, offers) Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)) — you may withdraw at any time
Improving our website and services through analytics Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)) — to develop and grow our business
Fraud prevention and security Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)) — to protect our business and users
Compliance with legal obligations (e.g., tax, accounting) Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c))
Personalising your experience on our website Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)) — via cookie preferences

5. Disclosure to Third Parties

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data. We may share your data with carefully selected third parties in the following circumstances:

  • Service Providers: Trusted partners who provide IT, payment processing, email delivery, and customer support services on our behalf. They are bound by data processing agreements and may only process your data on our instructions.
  • Travel Partners: Where necessary to fulfil your booking, we share relevant details with accommodation providers, local guides, transport operators, and activity providers.
  • Analytics Providers: We use Google Analytics (Google LLC) to understand how visitors use our website. Google may process data in the United States. We have enabled IP anonymisation and signed appropriate data processing agreements.
  • Advertising Networks: We may use remarketing tools (e.g., Google Ads, Meta Ads) to show you relevant advertising. These services use cookies and may process data in countries outside the UK/EEA.
  • Legal Authorities: Where required by law, court order, or governmental authority, we may disclose your data to regulatory or law enforcement bodies.
  • Business Transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or part of our business, your data may be transferred to the new owner, subject to the same privacy protections.

All third-party service providers are required to take appropriate security measures to protect your personal data and are not permitted to use it for their own purposes.

6. International Data Transfers

Some of our third-party service providers operate outside the United Kingdom. Where we transfer personal data to countries that do not offer an equivalent level of data protection, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, such as:

  • UK International Data Transfer Agreements (IDTAs) or EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) where applicable.
  • Adequacy decisions by the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).
  • Binding Corporate Rules where applicable.

You may contact us at info@domain.com to request further information about these safeguards.

7. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to enhance your browsing experience, analyse site traffic, and deliver personalised content and advertising.

Types of Cookies We Use

  • Strictly Necessary Cookies: Essential for the website to function correctly. These cannot be disabled. They include session management and security cookies.
  • Performance & Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting anonymised usage data. We use Google Analytics for this purpose.
  • Functional Cookies: Remember your preferences and choices (such as language or region) to provide a more personalised experience.
  • Targeting & Advertising Cookies: Used to deliver relevant advertisements and track the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns. These may be set by third-party advertising networks.

You can manage your cookie preferences at any time through our Cookie Consent tool or your browser settings. Note that disabling certain cookies may affect website functionality.

For full details on the cookies we use, please read our Cookie Policy.

8. Data Retention

We retain your personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, including meeting legal, accounting, or reporting obligations.

  • Enquiry and contact form data: Retained for up to 24 months from the date of your last interaction with us, unless a booking is made.
  • Booking and customer data: Retained for 7 years following the completion of your travel, in accordance with UK tax and accounting requirements.
  • Marketing data: Retained until you unsubscribe or withdraw consent, plus 12 months for record-keeping purposes.
  • Technical and analytics data: Retained for up to 26 months in anonymised or aggregated form.
  • Cookie data: Varies by cookie type; see our Cookie Policy for specifics.

When personal data is no longer required, it is securely deleted or anonymised.

9. Your Rights Under UK GDPR

As a data subject under UK GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data. You may exercise any of these rights by contacting us using the details in Section 11.

  • Right of Access: You have the right to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you (a "Subject Access Request").
  • Right to Rectification: You may request that we correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"): You may request deletion of your personal data where there is no compelling reason for us to continue processing it.
  • Right to Restriction of Processing: You may request that we restrict the processing of your data in certain circumstances (e.g., while accuracy is contested).
  • Right to Data Portability: Where processing is based on consent or a contract and carried out by automated means, you may request a copy of your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to Object: You have the right to object to processing based on legitimate interests, including for direct marketing purposes. We will stop processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Where processing is based on your consent, you may withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.
  • Right not to be subject to automated decision-making: We do not use automated decision-making or profiling that produces legal or similarly significant effects.

We will respond to all verified requests within 30 days of receipt. In complex cases, we may extend this period by a further two months and will notify you accordingly. There is no charge for exercising your rights in most circumstances.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), the UK supervisory authority for data protection matters, at ico.org.uk or by calling 0303 123 1113.

10. Data Security

We take the security of your personal data seriously and have implemented appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect it against unauthorised access, loss, alteration, or disclosure. These measures include:

  • Secure Sockets Layer (SSL/TLS) encryption for all data transmitted via our website.
  • Access controls ensuring only authorised personnel can access personal data.
  • Regular security reviews and staff data protection training.
  • Secure data storage with reputable, UK/EEA-based hosting providers.
  • Procedures to detect, investigate, and report personal data breaches in accordance with ICO requirements (within 72 hours where applicable).

Despite these measures, no internet transmission is completely secure. If you believe your data has been compromised, please contact us immediately.
